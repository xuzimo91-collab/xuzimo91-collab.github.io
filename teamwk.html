MOVIE

Film, characterized by motion/moving images, is a visual art form used to simulate experiences that convey ideas, stories, perceptions, feelings, beauty, or atmosphere through the use of moving images. These images are typically accompanied by sound and, more rarely, other sensory stimulations. Film was born in 1888 with Lewis Prince's 1888 film, "Scenes from the Gardens of Roundhay." However, even before that, Eadweard Muybridge's 1878 film, "Sallie Gardner at a Gallop," is sometimes considered a forerunner of early cinema. Then, on December 28, 1895, the Lumière brothers publicly screened their short film in the basement of the Grand Café at 14 rue des Capuchins in Paris, France, bringing cinema to the public's attention.
